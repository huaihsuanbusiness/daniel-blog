# References

## Primary sources

1. DORA. *State of AI-assisted Software Development 2025*.
   - https://dora.dev/dora-report-2025/
   - Key idea used: AI acts as an amplifier; returns depend on the underlying organizational system.

2. Digital.ai. *18th State of Agile Report*.
   - https://digital.ai/resource-center/analyst-reports/18th-state-of-agile-report/
   - Key idea used: “The Adaptation Era”; Agile is adapting, not fading; outcomes, value and adaptability remain central.

3. Atlassian. *Atlassian research: AI adoption is rising, but friction persists*.
   - https://www.atlassian.com/blog/developer/developer-experience-report-2025
   - Key idea used: AI is saving time, but organizational inefficiencies remain a major drag.

4. Atlassian. *State of Teams 2025*.
   - https://www.atlassian.com/blog/state-of-teams-2025
   - Key idea used: knowledge workers and leaders still spend significant time searching for answers and dealing with friction.

5. Scrum.org. *AI Is Rewiring Scrum Teams, But Not Scrum*.
   - https://www.scrum.org/resources/blog/ai-rewiring-scrum-teams-not-scrum
   - Key idea used: AI changes how Scrum Teams work, but Scrum’s empirical core remains relevant.

6. Thoughtworks. *Spec-driven development: unpacking 2025 new engineering practices*.
   - https://www.thoughtworks.com/en-us/insights/blog/agile-engineering-practices/spec-driven-development-unpacking-2025-new-engineering-practices
   - Key idea used: better context, clearer specifications, and better constraints matter more in AI-assisted workflows.

## Authorial judgement

The article’s main judgement is not that Scrum is obsolete. It is that different ceremonies now deserve different treatment because AI has lowered the cost of information preparation more than it has lowered the cost of decision quality, validation and risk control.
